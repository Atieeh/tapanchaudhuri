Professor Tapan K. Chaudhuri, MD, FACP, FACNM, specialized in the application of nuclear physics in medicine and spiritualism, and earned his medical degree from the University of Calcutta.

He received his training in nuclear medicine from Yale University and the University of Iowa.

After serving as Assistant Professor of Radiology at the University of Iowa and as Associate Professor of Internal Medicine at Eastern Virginia Medical School (EVMS), he became a full professor of radiology at EVMS.

His research centered around radionuclide methods to diagnose patency of tear duct after surgery and to assess bladder dysfunction in case of prostate enlargement; early diagnosis of complications of diabetes affecting the stomach, gallbladder, and urinary bladder; and the effects of various drugs on stomach emptying.

He has authored and co authored roughly two hundred publications in various national and international medical and scientific journals, including several book chapters.
